What is LEO (Low Earth Orbit)?

LEO (Low Earth Orbit) refers to an orbit around Earth with an altitude between 160 km and 2000 km. Satellites in LEO typically travel at very high speeds, completing an orbit in about 90 to 120 minutes. This makes LEO ideal for applications like:

  • Earth observation and imaging (e.g., weather satellites).
  • Communication satellites (e.g., Starlink).
  • Scientific research (e.g., the International Space Station).

Advantages of LEO:

  • Lower latency for communication compared to higher orbits.
  • Reduced launch costs due to proximity to Earth's surface.
  • High-resolution imaging due to the closer distance to Earth.

However, satellites in LEO face challenges such as atmospheric drag and limited coverage area, requiring multiple satellites for global coverage.
