What is MEO (Medium Earth Orbit)?

MEO (Medium Earth Orbit) refers to an orbit around Earth with an altitude ranging from 2,000 km to approximately 35,786 km. Satellites in MEO have moderate orbital periods and are commonly used for applications like:

  • Navigation systems (e.g., GPS, Galileo, GLONASS).
  • Satellite-based augmentation systems for aviation and maritime.
  • Communication satellites for regional coverage.

Advantages of MEO:

  • Wider coverage area compared to Low Earth Orbit satellites.
  • Lower latency than Geostationary Earth Orbit (GEO) satellites.
  • Ideal for navigation systems with global or regional coverage.

Challenges of MEO:

  • Higher launch and maintenance costs than LEO satellites.
  • Requires robust systems to handle radiation exposure in this orbit.
